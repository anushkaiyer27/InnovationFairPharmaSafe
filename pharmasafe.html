What is PharmaSafe?

By signing up with PharmaSafe, you will either receive text updates or google calendar notifications regarding when you need to take your medication as well as renewal notifications from your local drug store and information about side effects and directions. Don't worry too much if you experience these side effects. These are normal when taking certain pharmaceutical products.

About the PharmaSafe Team

Anushka Iyer

Anushka is a highschool student in West Windsor, New Jersey. She is passionate about computer science and applying her programming skills to solve prevalent world issues. After volunteering at a health center in Trenton As a volunteer at Henry J. Austin Health Center in Trenton, NJ for the past two summers, I worked with patients to register them in the online portal so that they could easily access their medical reports and prescriptions. Patients would often ask me to clarify their prescriptions as they didn't understand them. While I wasn't able to do this, I did feel that there was a problem that needed to be addressed. Through my research, I learned that more than 50% of patients don't take medications as prescribed. This problem is compounded for patients with low income levels, low literacy levels and advanced age. This is due to several reasons - rushed or inadequate communication between providers and patients, complicated medication regime, confusion about adverse effects, care by multiple physicians etc. Information handed out by pharmacies about adverse reactions is also extremely lengthy and often is not read at all. Simplifying this information would mean that patients can benefit from their medications which will improve their general well being and health. Through my experience as a volunteer and through extensive research, I realized that clearer and simpler communication would be extremely beneficial to all patients, especially the most vulnerable members of our community when it came to medication adherence. Being healthy is critically important and I want people to get better, not just spend more money on medicines that may not help if they aren't taken on time or correctly.
